Description

The stickleback reference genome was generated from DNA libraries with a variety of different insert sizes, all constructed from a single female fish from Bear Paw Lake, Alaska (Jones et al. 2012 Nature 484: 55-61). This track shows (end) read placements from two large insert Bacterial Artificial Chromosome (BAC) clone libraries made from the reference individual. For more details about the libraries themselves, see the CHORI-213 and CHORI-215 information pages at BACPAC Resources. To build this sticklebrowser track, over three hundred thousand (end) sequence reads generated from BAC clones at Broad Institute were trimmed based on sequence quality (Pred), placed against the original gasAcu1 stickleback reference genome assembly using BLAT, and filtered to retain only those placements whose second-highest hit scores were significantly lower (<70%) than the highest hit score. These placements were then lifted to gasAcu1-4. The individual BAC (end) reads can also be found by searching in the NCBI Nucleotide Database for "CHORI-213" and "CHORI-215".

You can use the names of individual clones shown in the track to order physical copies of the corresponding BAC clones from the frozen CHORI-213 and CHORI-215 libraries. Clones from the reference stickleback libraries are maintained and distributed by BACPAC Resources Center.
