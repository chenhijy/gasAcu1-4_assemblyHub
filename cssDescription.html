Description

The degree of marine–freshwater divergence is quantified using a Cluster Separation Score statistic which measures average pairwise genetic distance between marine and freshwater groups after accounting for the average distance within each of the groups (see Methods and Supplementary Equation 1 in Jones et al. 2012 for more details). Intervals represented in this track have experienced strong (FDR < 0.05 or FDR < 0.02) parallel divergence between marine and freshwater populations. This is best illustrated by the pattern at the Ectodysplasin (EDA) locus, a key gene previously shown to control repeated evolution of armor plate differences between marine and freshwater sticklebacks (Colosimo et al. 2005). Here, the alternative ancient alleles have been repeatedly selected and driven to high frequency in marine and freshwater habitats. Analyses were performed on genomic windows of 2500bp, stepping by 500bp.

Methods

Data were calculated from Illumina 36bp single-end short-read whole genome sequencing of 21 individual threespine stickleback fish from globally sampled (Pacific and Atlantic basins) marine and freshwater populations -- originally in gasAcu1 coordinates, which have been lifted to gasAcu1-4. For a detailed description of methods refer to Jones et al. 2012.
